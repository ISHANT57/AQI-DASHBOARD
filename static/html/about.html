Understanding Our Mission

Providing accurate and accessible air quality data to promote environmental awareness and public health.

Our Vision

At the Global Air Quality Dashboard, we believe that access to accurate air quality information is essential for public health and environmental decision-making. Our platform is designed to make complex air quality data accessible, understandable, and actionable for everyone.

Air pollution is one of the greatest environmental health threats of our time, contributing to millions of premature deaths annually. By providing comprehensive visualization tools, we aim to raise awareness about air quality issues and empower individuals, communities, and policymakers to take informed action.

About the Data

Our dashboard utilizes the Air Quality Index (AQI), a standardized indicator developed by environmental agencies to communicate how polluted the air is and what associated health effects might be a concern. The AQI focuses on health effects that may be experienced within a few hours or days after breathing polluted air.

AQI Scale and Health Implications

Good (0-50)

Air quality is considered satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk.

Moderate (51-100)

Air quality is acceptable; however, there may be a moderate health concern for a very small number of people.

Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups (101-150)

Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects. The general public is less likely to be affected.

Unhealthy (151-200)

Everyone may begin to experience health effects; members of sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects.

Very Unhealthy (201-300)

Health warnings of emergency conditions. The entire population is more likely to be affected.

Hazardous (301+)

Health alert: everyone may experience more serious health effects.

How to Use This Dashboard

  1. Explore the map: Use the City View to see AQI levels across different locations.
  2. Track historical data: Check the Trends page to understand how air quality has changed over time.
  3. Compare regions: Use the comparison tools to see how different areas compare.
  4. Gain insights: Visit the Insights page for deeper analysis and patterns.
